This guide walks you through everything you need to know as a new restaurant tenant at Plaza Latina — from how and when to pay rent, to expressing your brand through signage, to making sure nothing falls through the cracks during move-in. Read through at your own pace, check off your tasks, and don't hesitate to reach out to management with any questions.

1
Rent Payment System

All rent payments must be made through the AppFolio tenant portal — no other payment methods are accepted. Rent is due on the 1st of each month. Receipts are accessible anytime through your AppFolio portal.

💡 Pro tip: Set up auto-pay directly in AppFolio so rent goes out automatically on the 1st — no reminders needed, total peace of mind.
2
Signage — Be Creative, Be You

Your sign is the first thing customers see. We encourage creativity — bold colors, unique fonts, neon, dimensional lettering, murals, and illuminated signage are all welcome. Here's what you need to know:

🎨

Design Freedom

Express your brand fully. Choose your own fonts, colors, materials, and style. Neon signs, hand-painted boards, illuminated boxes — all fair game.

📐

Size & Placement

Signs must fit within your designated fascia area. Dimensions are in your lease addendum. No signage may extend beyond your unit boundaries.

✅

Approval Required

Submit a design mockup (PDF or image) to management for written approval before ordering or installing. Turnaround is typically 3–5 business days.

🚫

What's Not Allowed

Flashing strobe lights and signs on shared walls or the parking lot are not permitted.

🔧

Installation

Use a licensed sign contractor. Proof of insurance is required before installation. Coordinate with management on timing to avoid utility conflicts.

🌟 We want your storefront to stand out. Distinctive, well-designed signage benefits the whole plaza. Don't be shy — make it memorable. Just remember: written landlord approval is required for any sign on the exterior or common areas, and installation must be done by a licensed contractor.
3
Rules & Guidelines
Topic Summary
Operating Hours Thu & Mon 11AM–8PM · Fri–Sun 10AM–8PM. Must remain open all building hours — repeated violations may result in eviction.
Closure Notice Post a sign on premises and notify landlord in writing within 3 business days of any closure. Emergencies excluded.
Advertising Advertising payment is mandatory and based on square footage. Rent will not be accepted without the advertising payment — non-payment constitutes default.
Parking Park at outer lot edges only. Register all vehicles with landlord. No double parking — violators will be towed.
Conduct No smoking, loud music, or nuisance activity. Disruptive work requires 24-hr written notice. Children must be supervised at all times.
Safety No hazardous materials without a permit. No animals without written consent (seeing-eye dogs excepted).
Maintenance Keep your space clean. Use licensed contractors for all repairs. Tenant holds landlord harmless for maintenance-related incidents.
Health Permits & Licensing As a restaurant operator, you are fully responsible for obtaining all required city and county health permits before opening. This includes food handler certifications, food establishment permits, and any applicable fire or ventilation inspections. Management does not handle licensing on your behalf — start this process early as timelines vary.
Food Safety All food preparation and storage must comply with local health codes at all times. Post your health permit in a visible location inside the restaurant as required by law.
Grease & Waste Use designated dumpsters only. Grease trap installation and regular maintenance are the tenant's responsibility — keep all service records on file. Improper grease disposal is a lease violation.
Subletting Not permitted without written consent from management.
